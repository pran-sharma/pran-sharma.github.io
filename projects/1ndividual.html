Concept

Windows gaming is a massive, ever-expanding space, yet native support for controller configuration and calibration is extremely limited. This project's goal is to allow one to customize the functionality of their controllers through the Windows settings. Desirable, accessible features can be seen in Valve's Steam Controller or Microsoft's Xbox Adaptive Controller but require additional software to configure.

Potential Features

Users could remap inputs to output muti-key macros or multiple input combinations to output alternate behavior, or even swap between different configuration profiles to allow more functionality from the same limited inputs on a controller. Outputs may also be mapped to other accessibility-improving functionality, such as a controller-compatible on-screen keyboard or speech-to-text dictation. These features could even boost productivity in professional applications, where a user may desire more shortcuts to enrich and speed up their workflow.

The Steam Controller's on-screen keyboard allows one to type very effectively with a controller, with two cursors controlled by the left and right joystick and activated by the left and right trigger. Predictive text options allow for even quicker typing.

Conclusions

Handheld gaming devices such as Valve's Steam Deck may also benefit from a Windows controller configurator. More widespread game compatibility would be supported by running Windows on such devices, and full-fledged computer use could be enabled as well.

Enabling controllers to be used as primary inputs has great implications for accessibility, including the Xbox Adaptive Controller for near infinite customizability, as well as for Windows on handheld devices, such as the Steam deck.
