Autonomous Robotic Lawn Mower

Prelude

My senior year of undergraduate study quickly came around in my third year of college, and with that came my senior design capstone project. Despite having a fair bit of practical experience with robotics, from LEGO to metal, from friendly competition to national defense, I hadn't quite figured out what goals most motivated me, and without a clear destination in mind, the path to take was even less clear. Therefore, rather than taking matters into my own hands, I decided to listen to pitches and try to judge which projects most piqued my interest. A few computer vision projects were very intriguing, including monitoring analog gauges and representing their values digitally, and extracting characters, especially those difficult to read, from historic tombstones. However, I was most compelled by Duke Energy's pitch to automate their solar field's lawn maintenance with the power of robotics.

Concept

We were tasked to devise an efficient and precise method of autonomously cutting grass in a 500 square foot area within a field of solar panels with a robot no larger than two feet long in any dimension.
