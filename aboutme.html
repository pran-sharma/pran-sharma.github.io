Milestones of My Life

My First Dream Job

The first job I can remember ever wanting was to be an inventor when I was just beginning school.

Intro to Robotics

By middle school, I had a posse of little nerds like me, participating in math competitions and all. Some of my pals had the idea to join a Lego robotics league, so we teamed up and started competing. I loved the creative problem solving involved, and began down a long, fun path.

Choosing Computers

In high school, I joined the school's budding robotics and programming teams, finding fulfillment from learning new skills, solving interesting problems, developing strong relationships with my teammates, and taking on leadership responsibilities. Robotics introduced me to new technical skills, including CAD, 3D printing, laser cutting, even machining, massively widening my repertoire from the simple power tools my dad let me use growing up, but both teams improved my coding ability. I decided with my strengths, along with my personal interests and hobbies like graphic design and gaming, that a career in computers would be ideal.
